Frequently Asked Questions

  • What is the benifts of automation ?

    There are several benefits of automation, including:Increased efficiency: Automation can help streamline processes and tasks, reducing the time and effort required to complete them. This can lead to faster and more efficient operations, which in turn can increase productivity and profitability. Improved accuracy: Automated systems are typically more precise and consistent than human operators, which can lead to fewer errors and improved quality control. Cost savings: By reducing the need for manual labor, automation can help reduce labor costs and potentially lower the cost of goods and services. Enhanced safety: Automating certain tasks can help reduce the risk of injury or accidents in the workplace, particularly in hazardous or high-risk environments. Scalability: Automated systems can be easily scaled up or down to meet changing demand or business needs, which can help businesses remain agile and responsive. Data insights: Automated systems can collect and analyze data more quickly and accurately than humans, providing valuable insights that can inform decision-making and improve business processes.Overall, automation can help businesses operate more efficiently, effectively, and safely, while also reducing costs and improving the bottom line.

  • Can we convert manual plant to automatic ?

    In many cases, it is possible to convert a manual plant to an automatic one. However, the complexity and feasibility of such a conversion will depend on a number of factors, including the type of plant, the specific processes involved, the equipment and systems currently in place, and the level of automation desired.

  • What is the most common problems in automation ?

    Some of the most common problems in automation include:Technical Issues,Cost,Trained Staff,Integrations,Adaptability. Overall, while automation can bring many benefits to businesses, it is important to carefully consider these common challenges and plan for their mitigation when implementing automated systems.

  • What is the main steps for plant designing process ?

    The plant design process can vary depending on the specific industry and plant type, but some of the main steps typically involved in plant design include:

    • Project Planning
    • Site Evaluation
    • Conceptual Design
    • Detailed Design
    • Equipment Procurement
    • Construction and Installation
    • Testing and Commissioning
    • Operation and Maintenance
  • How is a PLC different from relay?

    A relay cannot be programmed but the PLC can be programmed, relay is not advanced like PLC. PLC can work with analog I/O’s whereas the relay cannot do this.

  • What is the difference between a DCS system, a SCADA system, and a PLC system?

    Distributed control system (DCS) is a computerized system used to control industrial processes that span a large geographical area. SCADA supevisory control and data acquisition) system is a computerized monitoring and controlling system for industrial processes. Programmable logic controller (PLC) is a computer system used to manage repetitive industrial processes.
